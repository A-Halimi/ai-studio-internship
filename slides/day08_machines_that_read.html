The big reveal // under the hood

ChatGPT's whole trick? Predict the next word. A trillion times.

No understanding is programmed in. It reads what's there and guesses what comes next — then adds that word and guesses again.

Do that fast enough, on enough text, and something that looks a lot like thinking falls out. That's the mystery we unpack today.

Concept 1 // how a model sees text

Models don't read letters or words.
They read tokens — puzzle pieces.

  • Before anything else, text is chopped into common chunks. Frequent words are one piece; rare ones shatter into many.
  • This is why GPT can't count the r's in strawberry — it never sees the letters. It sees two chunks.
word → tokens · the model's real input
internship→internship
strawberry→strawberry
KAUST→KAUST
2048→2048

Rare words & numbers fracture — that's why LLMs fumble spelling and arithmetic.

Concept 2 // meaning as geometry

Every token becomes an arrow in 768-D space.
Meaning = position.

a 2-D shadow of that space · similar words sit together
  • Near = similar. dog, cat and puppy huddle together; pizza sits far away.
  • Directions carry meaning. The jump from man→king is the same jump as woman→queen. The model can literally do arithmetic on meaning.
Concept 3 // the whole secret

For every next word, the model ranks all 50,000 tokens.

P(next token) · "The best thing about science is ___"
temperature →
  • It doesn't pick one answer — it assigns a probability to every token, then samples.
  • Temperature = how adventurous the pick is. Low: always the safe favorite. High: surprising, creative, riskier. Watch the bars.
Concept 4 // the trick that made it work

To guess well, each word must decide:
which other words should I look at?

"The penguin ate the fish because it was hungry."

What does it refer to — the penguin or the fish? You knew instantly. The model has to look back and weigh every earlier word.

That looking-back is attention

Every word builds a weighted glance at every other word. Change one word and the glances re-aim. On the next slide, you drive it.

Interactive // attention playground

arc thickness = how hard that word attends
 

Now flip hungry → delicious: the word "it" changes its mind — penguin was hungry, but the fish was delicious.

The code // three lines, two superpowers

You won't train these. You'll drive them.

from transformers import pipeline

# the next-word engine: GPT-2, 124M parameters
gen = pipeline("text-generation", model="gpt2")
gen("The best thing about science is",
    max_new_tokens=60, temperature=0.9)

# a mood reader, fine-tuned on movie reviews
mood = pipeline("text-classification",
                model="distilbert-base-uncased-finetuned-sst-2-english")
mood("This internship is secretly amazing.")
# → [{'label': 'POSITIVE', 'score': 0.9998}]
